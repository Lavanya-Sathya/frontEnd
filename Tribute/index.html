Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Here's a time line of Dr. Kalam's life:

  • 1931 Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu
  • 1940-1950 - Completed his schooling in Rameswaram and later in Ramanathapuram.
  • 1954 - Graduated in physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1955 - Enrolled at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT) to study aerospace engineering.
  • 1960 - After graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960, Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation.
  • 1965 - Kalam had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO
  • 1969 - Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Worked on India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III. Contributed to the development of India's first satellite, Aryabhata.
  • 1970s - Worked on the development of the Rohini Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV).
  • 1980s - Played a key role in the successful launch of Rohini Satellite. Appointed as the Director of Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS).
  • 1990s - Appointed as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India (1992-1999). Continued to contribute to India's space and defense programs. Worked on the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP).
  • 1998 - Significant contribution to India's nuclear tests at Pokhran-II ("Operation Shakti").
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India on July 25, 2002.
  • 2007 - Completed his presidential term in July 2007.
  • Post-Presidential Career - Continued to inspire students and youth through lectures and interactions. Authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds."
  • 2015 - Passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
  • Awards and Recognitions - Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor, in 1997. Received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to science, technology, and education.
  • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam's life and career were marked by his significant contributions to India's space and defense programs, his role in India's nuclear tests, and his inspirational leadership as the President of India. He continued to inspire and educate young minds even after his presidency, leaving a lasting legacy in the fields of science, technology, and education.

"Dr. Kalam was a great scientist, a wonderful President of India, and above all, a true patriot. He inspired millions of people, particularly the youth of India."

-- Former Indian Prime Minister Manmohan Singh
